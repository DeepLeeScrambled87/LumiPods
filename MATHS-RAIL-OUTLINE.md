# Maths Rail Outline

This is the first foundational rail to build.

## Role In LumiPods

The maths rail is a continuous foundation layer that:
- reinforces core mathematical thinking daily or near-daily
- adapts to learner level and confidence
- connects maths to the current thematic pod
- uses real tools, models, simulations, and projects

The goal is not memorization alone.
The goal is fluency, pattern recognition, modelling, application, and confident problem solving.

## Core Design Principles

- Maths must feel usable, visible, and powerful.
- Younger learners should lock in the basics through patterns, visuals, games, and concrete models.
- Older learners should use maths in science, ML, data, simulations, coding, and decision making.
- Every concept should include practical use cases.
- Tool use matters: notebooks, spreadsheets, Colab, Python, SQL, Jupyter, visual simulators, and AI-assisted exploration.

## Rail Structure

The maths rail should support both:
- continuous support-rail sessions
- standalone future main maths pods

That means each core maths area should become:
- a reusable concept spine for the rail
- a future pod family when selected as the main focus

## Core Maths Areas

1. Core Number Basics
- counting and place value
- arithmetic
- fractions, decimals, percentages
- ratio foundations

2. Pre-Algebra and Basic Algebra
- factors, multiples, primes, divisibility
- variables and simple equations
- ratios, rates, proportional reasoning

3. Geometry and Measurement
- perimeter, area, volume
- angles, shapes, coordinates
- scale, distance, spatial reasoning

4. Intermediate Algebra
- expressions and factoring
- systems of equations
- quadratics and functions

5. Trigonometry
- sine, cosine, tangent
- triangles and angles
- periodic behaviour and waves

6. Functions and Pre-Calculus
- function families
- sequences and series
- intuitive limits

7. Calculus
- rates of change
- accumulation
- optimization

8. Linear Algebra
- vectors and matrices
- systems
- eigen concepts and decomposition

9. Probability and Statistics
- probability
- distributions
- descriptive and inferential statistics
- regression

10. Discrete Mathematics
- logic and sets
- combinatorics
- graph theory
- algorithmic reasoning

11. Differential Equations and Dynamical Systems
- changing systems over time
- stability
- simple modelling

12. Real Analysis and Advanced Calculus
- rigorous foundations
- convergence
- multivariable thinking

13. Abstract Algebra and Number Theory
- modular arithmetic
- primes
- structures used in crypto and theory

14. Topology and Advanced Geometry
- continuity and connectedness
- manifolds and advanced spatial reasoning

  - introductory modelling

- Advanced

  - data analysis

- Pro

## Session Types

The rail should rotate through:
- concept mini-lesson
- worked strategy lab
- visual model or manipulation task
- quick check or flashcard review
- applied project mini-step
- tool-use studio
- reflection or explain-it-back
