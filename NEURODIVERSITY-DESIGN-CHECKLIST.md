# Neurodiversity Design Checklist

LumiPods is intentionally designed for both neurodivergent and non-neurodivergent learners.
The core aim is not to "accommodate later" but to build a learning environment that assumes learner variability from the start.

This direction is informed by:
- lived homeschooling experience
- project-based learning
- Universal Design for Learning (UDL)
- structured teaching principles
- ADHD-friendly pacing and transition support

The heart of the product is joyful, strategic, real-world learning that helps children feel capable, seen, and proud of how they think.

## Core Principles

- Short learning blocks beat long passive lessons.
- Pause, movement, reset, and resume are part of learning, not failures in learning.
- Understanding should lead to making, explaining, building, testing, and reflecting.
- Visual structure lowers cognitive load and increases independence.
- Choice should exist inside safe rails, not as open-ended chaos.
- Progress should be visible, encouraging, and grounded in real work.

## Already Present In LumiPods

- short, age-aware session blocks
- visible schedule structure
- movement and physical breaks
- pause and resume support
- project-based learning and portfolio building
- artifact capture and evidence of learning
- progress tracking across sessions
- smart schedule planning across weeks and months
- real-world, applied learning direction rather than memorization alone

## Pod Design Checklist

Use this when creating or reviewing a pod.

- Every pod should follow a predictable shape: warm-up, learn, do, reflect.
- Every session should have one clear learner-facing goal.
- Every session should be chunked into small, concrete steps.
- Every pod should end in artifacts, evidence, or a meaningful output.
- Every week should offer applied work, not just content intake.
- Every pod should support multiple levels without breaking the same conceptual spine.
- Every pod should include visuals, examples, and practical anchors.
- Every pod should include choice, but only in bounded amounts.
- Every pod should connect foundational knowledge to use cases, systems thinking, and problem solving.

## Learner Experience Checklist

- Timers should be visible and never punitive.
- Learners should always know what to do first, next, and when they are done.
- Instructions should be short, literal, and easy to re-open.
- Sessions should support re-entry after interruption.
- Layouts should stay consistent across the learner view.
- Noise should stay low: avoid clutter, unnecessary motion, or too many simultaneous actions.
- Reflection should stay light and supportive.
- Encouragement should focus on effort, strategy, and growth.

## Expression And Evidence Checklist

- Learners should be able to show understanding in more than one way.
- Acceptable outputs can include notes, photos, diagrams, builds, recordings, or short written responses.
- Projects should be broken into manageable steps with clear success signals.
- Portfolio evidence should be easy to upload, revisit, and celebrate.

## High-Value Additions Queued For Later

These are strong improvements, but not required to preserve the current structure.

- session checklists inside each block popup
- weekly encouragement summary tied to real achievements and badges
- first / next / done strip in learner session views
- one-tap post-session focus or difficulty self-rating
- calm-mode toggle for lower visual noise

## Guardrails

Avoid adding features that create clutter or extra pressure.

- do not overload sessions with too many choices
- do not add long reflection forms after every block
- do not rely on animation as a substitute for clarity
- do not make badges louder than the learning itself

## Product Intention

LumiPods should help children build confidence, independence, and creative problem-solving ability.
Different kinds of minds should feel supported without being singled out.
The product should make excellent learning feel possible, practical, and deeply human.
